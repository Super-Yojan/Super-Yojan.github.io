The Project

In my Enginerring 107 class for my final project I was supposed to create a platform that carries 2.5kg package from the drone delivered location to the front of door step.

Scenario

You work at a recently amalgamated company, Acme Paper Science Corporation (APSC), now the largest manufacturer of paper products on the US East Coast. You are a design engineer of the newly established Research and Development (R&D) Group tasked with developing a range of new paper products to address the needs of a rapidly developing drone delivery market segment. In this case, a major problem with drone deliveries has been identified that your CEO demands immediate attention – delivered goods are sitting out in the sun or rain after delivery, resulting in significant customer dissatisfaction. The Chief Engineer of the R&D Group has decided that your small team is to investigate and develop a prototype product to move the delivered product from the drone drop off point to the front door of the home to which it was delivered. A different team is working on the drone drop off point accuracy and has determined that they can deliver the package to within 2m of the front door safely. They have figured out how to place the package so that it is optimally oriented to the front door. Furthermore, they have developed the mechanism to connect the delivery vehicle to the drone such that it will be optimally placed and oriented such that it can take the package from the drone drop off location to the front door with the minimum of guidance technology required, thus keeping costs down. Your job is to design the vehicle to take the package from the drone drop off location to the front door and deposit the package on the first doorstep.

Task

Construct a platform to carry a ~2.5-kilogram sack of loose material (e.g. sugar or flour) at least 2m over a floor/flat surface. Once at the front door, the platform is to elevate the package to place it on the doorstep (approximately 0.2m above the ground). The specifications of this delivery platform are detailed below.

Specification and Constraints

  • Our investors want us to use our primary product as the basis for our platform – paper. We maximize our profitability by using paper. We don’t want to enhance our competition’s profitability by using their products (wood) in our design. See the list of Allowed Materials for those that are identified as suitable for this project.
  • Our product must be as lightweight as possible. This will ensure that delivery drones do not use up valuable battery life by carrying our delivery platform on board. Reduced battery life means fewer delivering and a shorter range for deliveries, both undesirable outcomes for delivery services. a. Priority (i.e. higher grades) will be given to lighter platforms. In the case of two equally capable platforms, the lighter platform will be allocated more utility (and thus a higher grade).
  • We are designing a platform for a 2.5 kg package, approximately 20cm x 12cm x 8cm.
  • The front doorstep is ~20cm above the floor or ground. The step face is vertical, and the top of the step extends a minimum of 30 cm from the vertical face.
  • The floor/ground between the drone drop off point and the front door is flat.
  • The package is oriented towards the front doorstep at the drone drop off point, and the delivery platform is optimally oriented.
  • The platform does not have to perform any further task once the package is on the front step.

Allowed Materials

  1. Paper
  2. Paper clips x 16 (max)
  3. Pencil
  4. Paper glue
  5. 1 x 3D-printed part or assembly
  6. Electric motor (includes maximum of 1 x battery)
  7. Rubber band(s)
  8. Plastic straws x 4
  9. Wheels (any size or materials) x 4 maximum
  10. String
  11. 1 x 3D-printed device or assembly

Solution

With the given constraints, we first had to figure out how to move the 2.5kg package from Point A to Point B. We can only use one motor, and one battery.
